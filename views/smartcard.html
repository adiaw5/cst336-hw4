What is a smart card authentication?


A smart card is a secure microcontroller that is capable of storing digital certificates, generating and operating on cryptographic keys. A smart card is used by corporations as a mechanism of authentication for employees and partners. Typically, users connect their smart card to a host computer which via a software, interacts with the keys material and other secrets stored on the smart card to authenticate the user. Smart cards are the most secure type of authentication for several reasons. First off, a pin is required to unlock them with a user-PIN prior to operation. In addition smart cards are considered a very strong form of authentication because cryptographic keys and other secrets stored on the card are very well protected both physically and logically, and are therefore extremely hard to steal with tamper resistance. The added security provided by the smart card comes at the expense of the user experience, because the user is required to carry around his or her smart card. In addition the smart card must be inserted into the computer each time the user needs to authenticate and must be removed when away from the computer for security purpose which can be a burden for the user. Furthermore, users are limited to host devices that have the card interface software installed.
